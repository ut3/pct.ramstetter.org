Day 23: Leaving Big Bear (Again)

May 13, 2015 Rick AKA Apollo LinkedIn Home One comment

Our 23rd day on the Pacific Crest Trail. We left our hotel via a ride from trail angel Mountain Mama. We resumed hiking at Van Dusen Canyon road.

—

May 11th, 2015

Start location: Van Dusen Canyon road, mile 275
End location: mile 292, at a campsite between a jeep road (3N16) and a large creek (Holcomb Creek)

We woke early, especially by slept-in-a-hotel standards. By 8am we had breakfast and coffee. By 9am we were packed. Our ride from Mountain Mama came at 10am- we’d prearranged the ride the night before. We shared the ride with two other hikers, both of whom were also headed up Van Dusen Canyon road. One of them worked at an outfitter and had lots of opinions about Rick’s still-new-on-the-market Six Moon Designs pack that we didn’t really care to hear.

We cleared Van Dusen road very quickly in Mountain Mama’s Nissan Pathfinder– clearly a superior off road vehicle to a Yaris. We helped Mountain Mama do some trivial work on a water cache she maintains. By 11:15am we were hiking.

We hiked through pine groves that were very, very tall. A large fire destroyed many trees in the area, but throughout the day we saw trees so tall that they survived the fires: their bases and trunks adapted to surviving fire.

Tall pine trees whose trunks were scorched. They survived a major fire.

Around 1pm we stopped for lunch at a great spot. We had a panoramic view of Big Bear (the lake), Big Bear Lake (the city), and Big Bear City (the unincorporated area). The spot was shady and had a large log for us to sit on.

Lunch spot overlooking Big Bear Lake

We had views of the lake throughout the day.

Around 3pm we fetched water from Holcomb Creek where Coxey Truck Trail crosses the PCT. It was there that we got our first weird comment of the day: another hiker, completely straight-faced and serious, wanted to know if we’d be eating breakfast or lunch at the Cajon Pass McDonald’s in 3 days. We responded with “no clue” and, in response, got a curt “you’ve still got a few days to figure it out.” This gentleman was truly under the impression that he knew exactly which meal he’d be eating at the Cajon Pass McDonald’s in 3 days.

Most of our day was spent in the burn area.

Everything burnt just after Big Bear Lake on the PCT

After that, we hiked through desert-like terrain with sand and boulders. The trail in that area was often narrow, dug into the side of ever-eroding hills made of sand.

Eventually we dropped into a canyon with dense forest and a nicely flowing creek (Holcomb Creek). It is in that canyon that we’ve made camp for the evening. As we finished making camp, a couple we hadn’t seen before walked by. They congratulated us (literally, “congrats guys”) on having made a campsite. This was the day’s second odd exchange with other hikers.

High point in the valley in which we made camp

We saw many other hikers throughout the day, but we hadn’t met any of them before.

We’re camped next to a creek, and the sound of flowing water is lulling us both to sleep. Goodnight.

 

Pinecones love poop.
