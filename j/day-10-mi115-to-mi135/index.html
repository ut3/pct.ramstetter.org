Day 10: mi115 to mi135

May 1, 2015 Rick AKA Apollo LinkedIn Home 2 comments

Our tenth day on the Pacific Crest Trail. Trail magic (burgers!) at trail angel Mike Herrera’s oasis. Rick/Apollo is battling blisters.

Start location: Agua Caliente creek at mile 115
End location: mile 135 at a 3-tent site right off the trail
Miles hiked: 20
April 28th, 2015

We woke early-ish and were walking by 8am. We had to start our day carrying the maximum amount of water we’re currently capable of; the next water source was 14 miles away.

Our start was slowed by Rick tending to blisters. They have developed, at least for Rick, in the same places they existed early on the AT. It’s very hard to find hiking shoes for feet that REI shoe salesmen have called “the widest I’ve ever seen,” and it is clear from the location of all blisters that Rick’s “wide” sized Merrell shoes aren’t wide enough. Yet these Moab Ventillator Low-rise shoes are one of the widest hiking shoes made.

Rick tending to a blister

We climbed out of the canyon through which Aqua  Caliente creek runs. Our views were expansive with fair, moderate weather in the AM. The plant life quickly changed from creek-side trees to tough, short desert shrubs. We passed multiple boulder fields, with boulders often exceeding the size of a car.

Desert view from the Pacific Crest Trail

Red desert flowers along the Pacific Crest Trail

Purple desert flowers along the Pacific Crest Trail

We passed a mountain with a fire tower on it; another hiker called it “Hot Springs” mountain but we’re skeptical that name is correct.

We leapfrogged other groups repeatedly throughout the day. Our early morning was ascending, our late morning was descending.

We had lunch at Mike Herrera’s desert oasis. This is a jeep-road accessible vacation home within a few hundred feet of the PCT. The property is entirely off the grid, with a well and solar panels. The owner, Mike Herrera, graciously lets hikers use the property.

Mike Hererras oasis

People keep giving us cheeseburgers

To our surprise, “PCT Tom” was there making cheeseburgers for passing hikers. This was the third time PCT Tom has fed us- he was the one at Kickoff making delicious fried tacos.

Hikers we previously met, including the girls we hitchhiked to Kickoff with, soon arrived. We met another married couple, exactly our age and from the San Francisco area.

We had arrived at Mike’s oasis around 1:30pm, and we left at 3pm. The couple we’d just met left around the same time, and we leapfrogged them throughout the afternoon.

The afternoon consisted of major descending. Views and plantlife continued as they had been in the morning.

Desert view from the Pacific Crest Trail

Pink desert flowers along the Pacific Crest Trail

Yellow flowers along the Pacific Crest Trail

We took a break around 5pm to change socks. It’s a habbit that we need to impose on ourselves, especially Rick- dry socks mean fewer blisters.

At 6:30pm we stopped walking. Us, the other couple, and another slightly older couple are all camping together at mile 135. The slightly older couple showed up late, around 7:30pm. At 9:45pm another group showed up and woke us up.

Mile 135 tent site and its view

Mile 135 tent site and its view

Goodnight.
