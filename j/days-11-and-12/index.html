Days 11 and 12

May 1, 2015 Starbuck AKA Erica LinkedIn Leave a comment

Our 11th and 12th days on the PCT. We hitchhiked to Idyllwild and took a zero day there.

Day 11 (April 29)

Started at: Mile 135, 2 miles south of Tule Spring

Stopped at: Idyllwild, CA (trail mile 152)

Miles hiked: 17

Miles hitchhiked: 19

–Started walking early (7 am), before the other two couples we camped with had even had breakfast.

–Got to Tule Spring (2 miles north and .25 miles off trail) within an hour.

Trail Fork for Tule spring

–Filled up on water in preparation for a ~15-mile hot and dry stretch without any guaranteed water sources.

— Passed a terrible water source, guzzler, with dead animals floating in it.

“Guzzler”: terrible water source with dead animals in it

–Very warm, sunny and exposed hiking all day

Short shrubs mean no shade. At all.

–Took shorter, more frequent breaks to get out of the relentless sun.

–Saw very few hikers on the trail; most people, like us, were definitely feeling the heat.

–Passed an unmaintained water cache with very little water left.

Unmaintained water cache: misguided trail magic

–Erica got an unpleasant sunburn on her calves, despite sunblock.

–Reached highway 74 in the late afternoon and immediately got a hitchhike into Idyllwild from a man named Alan. He had come up the mountain from Palm Desert specifically to drive hikers around town.

Alan, our hitchike from sr74 to Idyllwild

–Got one of the last rooms at the Silver Pines Lodge for a decent hiker rate.

–Had pizza, salad, beer, and popcorn in the room while watching TV (oh, the luxury).

–Saw a number of other hikers around town, all staying in motels or at a nearby campground.

–Went to bed “late” (after 10pm!).

Idyllwild is beautiful

Day 12 (April 30)

Started at: Idyllwild

Stopped at: Idyllwild

Miles hiked: 0

Miles hitchhiked: 0

 

–Restless sleep after too much pigging out before bed.

–Erica got the laundry started (hikers get a free load at the Silver Pines), picked up a sticky bun and cinnamon bun from the Town Baker (yum!), and got mail from the post office.

Giant cinammon roll and sticky bun from Idyllwild’s Town Baker

–Decided to keep the room for a second night (with an additional discount) in order to complete chores around town and relax a bit after the previous day’s long, hot hike.

–Rick spent much of the day using the local library’s computers to deal with financial chores. Hiking is expensive :(

–Erica made a few changes to the resupply strategy and spent most of the day running around town and getting stuff done (replacement washer for the water filter, Mother’s Day shopping, clothing augmentation to prevent future sunburn, more post office errands, and resupplying for the next stretch of trail).

–Spent the evening drinking wine on the motel room’s patio, followed by popcorn eating and TV watching.
