Day 22: Back to Big Bear

May 13, 2015 Rick AKA Apollo LinkedIn Home Leave a comment

Our 22nd day on the Pacific Crest Trail. We hiked from the PCT crossing with SR-18 to Van Dusen Canyon Road, where we were picked up by Rick’s mom (to celebrate Mother’s Day in Big Bear).

—-

May 10th
Start location: About a mile before the PCT intersection with SR-18– roughly at mile 265.
End location: Van Dusen Canyon Road, mile 275
Miles hiked: About 10

Our day started early. We were hiking by 8:30am. Like the day prior, scenery flip-flopped between grand pine trees and desert shrubs.

The hike between SR-18 and Van Dusen Canyon Road was short and unremarkable. We passed people walking southbound; they were slackpacking with the help of Papa Smurf and Mountain Mama (recall that we’d bumped into people doing the same the day prior, but south of the SR-18 intersection).

We passed an off-the-grid mansion whose private windmill emitted a loud, bassy hum.

off the grid mansion near Big Bear

People who visit the desert are often overtaken by an urge to shoot at things. This was true dangerously close to the sr-18 intersection.

Gunshot damage near sr-18

In this area, pine trees grew very tall- possibly the tallest yet. The trail was very rocky.

Rocky trail near Big Bear

We arrived at Van Dusen Canyon Road around 12:30pm. Upon arrival we discovered that the road was a jeep road. It being unpassable by my mother’s car, we decided to walk down the road towards Big Bear.

We walked no longer than two minutes before a car pulled up behind us and beeped. Apparently my mother and her boyfriend had driven up the wrong canyon roads (Holcom Canyon) and come down Van Dusen in her Toyota Yaris. An impressive feat indeed: offroad Yaris! While descending the mountain, there were multiple times where everyone had to exit the vehicle to prevent it from bottoming out.

We drove to Papa Smurf’s and Mountain Mama’s house to shower. They’re trail angels in the area (see above slackpacking) and were happy to let us shower.

About an hour later, everyone was seated at the Peppercorn Grille restaurant in Big Bear Lake. The food was good; the service was unreasonably slow given that the time was after lunch but before dinner (about 3pm).

After dinner we went to the brewery next door and had two flights of beer.

Rick’s mom took us on some errands, which lasted till about 6:15pm. By 6:30pm we were saying goodbye in the Robin Hood Resort parking lot.

Mother’s Day Good yes

Again, Erica and I had returned to the Robin Hood resort. It was convenient and cheap. And the staff was, again, very accommodating. For the second time, they brought a microwave to our room :)

We did laundry at the “Clean Jeans” laundromat. This was our second time there. Everything went smoothly the first time. On this second trip, the laundromat had shut off its water supply. We had to wait over an hour for the water to be turned back on-  our clothes locked inside a confused machine trying desperately to fill itself in the interim.

After our chores were complete, about 9:30pm, we indulged in chocolate cake and wine. A winning combination if ever there was one :)

Desert flowers early in the day.
