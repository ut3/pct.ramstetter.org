Day 21: Leaving Big Bear

May 13, 2015 Rick AKA Apollo LinkedIn Home One comment

Our 21st day on the Pacific Crest Trail. We hitchiked from our hotel room down SR-38 to the point at which we left the PCT a few days prior, then hiked on to the SR-18 crossing.

—-
May 9th
Start mile: near mile 251, a dirt road which leads to SR-38 directly across from LA County D.O.E.’s “Blue Sky Meadow” property.
End mile: near mile 265, just before the crossing with SR-18.
Miles hiked: about 14
Miles hitchhiked: About 30 (Big Bear Lake to Blue Sky Meadow property)

Our day started late. Around 9:30am we ate breakfast at Teddy Bear Restaurant for the second time in a few days. We thoroughly recommend that place; Teddy Bear restaurant in Big Bear rocks!

Checking out of Robin Hood Resort was pleasant. There was some confusion about whether or not we’d prepaid for our final night there (we hadn’t). Robin Hood Resort was incredibly friendly to us during our stay there. Hiker guidebooks (Yogi, Guthooks, Halfmile) don’t even mention the place. Our rate per night was comparable to the price of two bunks at the nearby Big Bear Lake hostel.

The staff at Robin Hood Resort took care of our every need during our stay there. On the first night of our stay, they brought us a microwave (to our room) and a wine bottle opener.

By 11:30am we were finally hitchhiking.  We did so from the parking lot of the gas station at Pine Knot and Big Bear Blvd. A local couple gave us a lift down SR-38. They seemed vaguely on edge and admitted that they don’t often stop for hitchhikers. (Erica is worth her weight in gold when it comes to hitchhiking –Rick)

We started walking by 12:15pm. The final 16 miles into Big Bear (or, rather, the final 16 miles before the crossing with SR-18) were easy. The only worrisome section involved walking on a narrow trail cut into the side of a mountain of rocks, with a ~100ft drop to your right.

We had great views of the Lucerne Valley area throughout the day.

Lucerne Valley as seen from the PCT near Big Bear

We arrived at the water source at mile 258 and filled up for a potential 15 mile dry stretch. While filling up, another hiker passed us.  He was the first hiker we’d seen all day.

We passed a dumpster sized metal crate in which local trail angels stock trail magic. It was the most professional unattended trail magic we’d seen. We later found out it is run by “Sarge” at the Big Bear Lake hostel.

Big metal crate of trail magic from Sarge near Big Bear Lake

By about 2pm we crossed from pine trees back into desert shrubs. We were both amazed that desert flora could exist so close to Big Bear Lake- we were only about 8 miles by plane away from Big Bear!

Scenery in the Big Bear , CA area. Who knew desert shrubs were so close (8 miles) to a ski resort?

We hiked on till about 6:15pm. We made camp just before SR-18’s crossing at mile 266. Our campsite had a view of Yucca Valley and Lucerne Valley below. As we made camp, two slackpacking hikers passed. Rick talked with one of them apparently the local trail angels Papa Smurf and Mountain Mama provide slackpacks. Rick fell asleep quickly that night; Erica stayed up reading.

Campsite with a view at mile 266
