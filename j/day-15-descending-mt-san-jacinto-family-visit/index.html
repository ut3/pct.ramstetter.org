Day 15: Descending Mt San Jacinto & Family Visit

May 8, 2015 Rick AKA Apollo LinkedIn Home Leave a comment

Our 15th day on the Pacific Crest Trail. We spent the morning and early afternoon descending Mt San Jacinto. In the late afternoon, we were met trailside by family.

Start location: Fuller Ridge Trailhead (and car camping), ~Mile 191
End location: Mile 206.8, Snow Canyon Road
Miles hiked: About 16

Our day started early, around 6:30am. The morning was loud– car campers have a tendency to set off car alarms. It was cold; in the 40s F. The morning was otherwise unevetful.

We were walking by 8am. By 8:40am we started getting views of the desert floor far, far below. As part of that view, we caught a glimpse of Morongo Casino. It hit us, then, that we should see if any family wanted to visit. We had cell service, and we called family. We made spur-of-the-moment plans to visit Erica’s family at Morongo.

The view that inspired our side trip to Morongo Casino

Descending Mt San Jacinto

Around 6,600feet altitude we started transitioning from pine groves to desert shrubs. The transition was gradual, with desert shrubs and pine trees existing side-by-side for a while.

The transition from pine groves to desert descending from Mt San Jacinto

The trail was very eroded in some parts, particularly those closer to the desert floor. It often wasn’t cut deep enough into the side of the mountain; one switchback level was collapsing into another.

We passed the 200 mile marker

The PCT leads off a cliff. (Not really).

Around 2pm Rick tripped on a rock and fell. It was a hard, face forward fall, induced by a toe-trip. Rick got a few scrapes, but was lucky to have avoided any major injury or gear destruction.

At the base of the mountain we arrived at our first water source since the day prior. It’d been 20 miles. This water source is provided by a local water district/company. That company has a large water storage tank, and a runoff pumping plant, a bit up the side of Mt San Jacinto. A water main runs from that tank+plant to the town below. This water source was a high-school-style water fountain tapped into that water main. Someone had erected a tarp (for shade) around the water fountain. Potentially a different someone had left large buckets of avacados and oranges there.

The tented area under which a desert water agency has tapped their water main and added a water fountain for hikers. The large pipe is the tapped water main.

Water district’s water main. They tapped a water fountain into the big pipe for hikers!

There were many other hikers there, everyone tired from the descent, and nobody particularly wanting to continue for the next ~6 miles to make it to a nearby hostel, Ziggy and Bear’s Hostel.

We did not stay long; we continued down the road (the PCT is a road walk here) and soon arrived at Erica’s parent’s car. By 5:30pm we were at Morongo, by 6:30pm we were showered and at the buffet line. It was great. Rick ate 4 plates of food; Erica ate 3.

Later that night, we did laundry at what may be the worst laundromat ever. H&H Coin Laundry in Banning, CA is a joke. Very few of the machines work. It took us three tries to find a machine that worked. We saw poor reviews on Google and Yelp, all of which say H&H’s machines don’t work. It is unfortunate that there are literally no other laundromats in the area.

By 9:30pm we were back from laundry. By 10pm we were asleep.
