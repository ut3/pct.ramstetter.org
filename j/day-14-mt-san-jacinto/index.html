Day 14: Mt San Jacinto

May 4, 2015 Rick AKA Apollo LinkedIn Home 2 comments

Our 14th day on the Pacific Crest Trail. We summited Mt San Jacinto and had great views of Palm Springs and Mt San Gregonio.

Start location: Mile 182
End location: Mile 191
Miles hiked: about 11 due to the Mt San Jacinto alternate route diverging from the PCT.

Our day started late. We were walking by about 8:45am. We immediately crossed from federal lands to state lands, the Mt San Jacinto State Wilderness.

Sign we passed warning of hazardous conditions on the way up to Mt San Jacinto

About 0.7miles from the federal/state boundary we filled up our water bladders. It took a long time due to the slow rate of flow from the spring. Rick was stung by a bee while pulling water from the spring.

The climb was steeper and more rugged than the Pacific Crest Trail (reminder: at this point we’re on the Mt San Jacinto Alternate, not the PCT). We had awesome views the entire way.

We hiked on to the summit, and there found a shelter that isn’t in any guidebooks. It’s an emergency shelter,  but hikers can use it.

Shelter at the summit of Mt San Jacinto

We ate lunch on the summit, and were disappointed by the number of day hikers at the peak. Evidently, many had taken the Palm Springs Tram nearly to the top. There were at least 50 people on the summit with us; it wasn’t ecologically friendly.

The view from the summit was panoramic to about 300 degrees and well worth the climb. At over 10,000ft, Mt San Jacinto is easily higher than anything on the Appalachian Trail.

Palm Springs to the Right, the fork of I-10 and SR-111 at left, and 29 Palms highway running north-south at center

After a long lunch, we started the climb down. Great views continued. The number of day hikers diminished after we took a fork towards Little Round Valley and Fuller Ridge.

Mt San Jacinto descent view

Mt San Jacinto descent view

Shortly after the Mt San Jacinto Alternate rejoined the PCT, we filled up on water again. This would be our last fill up of water for 20 miles. We left with a total of 13 liters of water (that’s 26 pounds of water across the two of us).

At around 8,300 feet during our descent, we passed through an atmospheric layer that netted a temperature increase of at least 10 degrees Fahrenheit over the course of about 100 trail feet.

As we descended, the pine trees grew more dense. They thinned with increasing elevation. We’ve been surrounded by pine trees since leaving Idyllwild. Only tomorrow will we (briefly) return to desert flora- we cross the desert floor through Cabazon, CA.

We hiked until about 6:30pm, and stopped at mile 191. There is a trailhead here, complete with car parking, for the Fuller Ridge trail. Camp was made and dinner eaten by 7:45.

There are other hikers here, one of whom took a liter of water from us at the summit. He ran short, and Rick drank less than normal. There’s a group of around 5 hikers, all in their early 20s, cowboy camping. They have no tents or bivy sacks. There’s also an older thru-hiker, who is somewhat deaf, that we leapfrogged throughout the afternoon.

There are many car campers here at mile 191. The Mt San Jacinto area is popular. Maybe one of the car campers will give us food in the morning ;)
